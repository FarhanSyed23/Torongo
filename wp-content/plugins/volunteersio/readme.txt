== Description ==
The easiest way to get volunteers for your tasks. They sign up/register themselves on your WP site/blog.
Fully integrated. No hassles. No need to change any PHP or CSS file!
Stop messing around with Google sheets or losing your visitors to anonymous third party platforms or lengthy bureaucratic procedures.

The Voluntueers.io for WordPress plugin connects to volunteers.io and seamlessly integrates a ssl secured service into your WordPress site.

== Installation ==

<h3>PLUG IN</h3>

1. Login to your WordPress admin panel, navigate to the Plugins menu and click Add New.
1. Search for 'Volunteers.io' and click at Install now. Volunteers.io will then be automatically downloaded and installed.
1. Activate the plugin through the 'Plugins' menu in WordPress.

<h3>Manual installation</h3>

1. Download `wp_volunteers.zip` from https://www.volunteers.io/wp_volunteers.zip to the `/wp-content/plugins/` directory.
1. Activate the plugin through the 'Plugins' menu in WordPress.

== Frequently Asked Questions ==

= I do not see any changes =

Please refresh your browser and/or empty the proxy/cache of WP.

= Where is the data saved? =

Your personal data is saved in your local WP database.
The settings (texts, css, preferences, etc) and sign up data (name and e-mail (encrypted)) are saved over an ssl secured connection on Amazon S3.
You can select what region of Amazon will be used.

= Where can I ask a question about Volunteers.io =

Please use the SUPPORT option at [Volunteers.io](http://www.volunteers.io "Ask a question").
We are happy to receive your feedback and/or to help you.
